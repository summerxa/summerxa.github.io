After Eve

Sep 2026-Present | Unity, C#, Game development

A 2D bullet hell roguelite and narrative game. Created by a team of student developers.

My main contributions:

  • Architect and implement several key systems, including optimized management of status effects in real time, a full inventory and shop system, and gameplay cycle integrated with narrative. Created an easy-to-understand and extensible system for future gameplay features.
  • Coordinate regularly with designers and other programmers to ensure smooth integration of each feature and maintain an organized codebase.
  • Contribute to art and narrative design meetings, providing regular updates about the behavior behind specific gameplay features and ensuring assets could be feasibly implemented in game.

Among other gameplay features, I was responsible for implementing the connection between the roguelite gameplay cycle and narrative scenes. A player should be able to start a new run, triggering unique dialogue according to their story progress, and then traverse through a fixed number of rooms, some procedurally generated and some fixed based on story progress. To achieve this, I needed to consolidate and combine the code for individual gameplay features from other programmers. While doing so, I communicated with the team members responsible for each part of the game to ensure combining or adding new features did not cause any regressions in existing functionality.

Additionally, this was the largest game project/team I had worked on so far, so a decent portion of my work involved coordinating with other programmers and explaining my implementations and design decisions at weekly meetings. In addition to meeting with the programming team, I participated in art and narrative design meetings, providing regular updates about how specific features were implemented in game and what assets were needed from the other teams.
