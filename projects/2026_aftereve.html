After Eve

Sep 2026-Present | Unity, C#, Game development

A 2D bullet hell roguelite and narrative game in progress, created by a team of student developers.

My main contributions:

  • Architect and implement several key systems, including optimized management of status effects in real time, a full inventory and shop system, and gameplay cycle integrated with narrative. Created an easy-to-understand and extensible system for future gameplay features.
  • Coordinate regularly with designers and other programmers to ensure smooth integration of each feature and maintain an organized codebase.
  • Participate in art and narrative design meetings, providing regular updates about the behavior behind specific gameplay features and ensuring assets could be feasibly implemented in game.

Among other gameplay features, I was responsible for implementing the connection between the roguelite gameplay cycle and narrative scenes. A player should be able to start a new run, triggering unique dialogue according to their story progress, and then traverse through a fixed number of rooms, some procedurally generated and some fixed based on story progress. Achieving this was a team effort: I not only needed to architect and implement the narrative management logic myself, but also consolidate and connect the work of the other team members. While doing so, I communicated with the team members responsible for each part of the game to ensure combining or adding new features did not cause any regressions in existing functionality.

Additionally, this was the largest game project/team I had worked on so far, so a decent portion of my work involved coordinating with other programmers and explaining my implementations and design decisions at weekly meetings. In addition to meeting with the programming team, I contributed to art and narrative design meetings, providing regular updates about how specific features were implemented in game and what assets were needed from the other teams.
